Ham slicing

Spanish-style cured ham deserves a professional skilled ham slicer.
‍
Thin-bladed knife help to delicate fibers of the ham, protect its texture, and pronounce the flavor.
‍
Look for the direction of the muscle fibers and make sure to cut along the grain.
‍
Thin is in!
Aim to cut thin, even slices. Thicker slices can overpower the delicate flavor of the ham.
‍

The rolling hills and verdant pastures, in Sevilla, provide the perfect setting for our pigs to live and thrive.
‍
With a warm, sunny climate and abundant natural resources, our pigs are able to roam freely and enjoy a varied diet of acorns (the most important effect), grasses, and other forages.

This free-range lifestyle results in a lean, muscular pig with a high percentage of unsaturated fat, which contributes to the rich, nutty flavor and tender texture of Iberian ham.
‍

Jabugo, Spain

In order to make the finest Iberian and fresh pork, we need a key piece of puzzle, Jabugo.

The region experiences relatively mild temperatures and high humidity, undergoing a unique Mediterranean climate, hot, dry summers and mild winters with around 15°C to 25°C & 50-80% of humidity.

From generation to generation, the process of traditional manufacturing makes Jabugo well-known in the world.
